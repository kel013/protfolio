Wonderland Confectionary

Trailer

Wonderland Confectionary is an open exploration game where you play as Ami, a baker of magical desserts. The player will explore an open space to collect ingredients to bake into desserts which they would give to customers. The main concept of the game is to allow players to explore where they want and find reward in the things that they find. From small caves to ridable flying fish, the player would find them by either exploring naturally or from the notes given by customers after finishing a request.

This was a two person project created by Ah Young Joo and myself.

I was primarily responsible for:
Design:
Both of us came up with the initial concept of the game. I was mostly responsible for creating and solidifying the main game loop, tutorial and request tree of the game.
To allow the player to learn the basic mechanics of explore, collect, bake and turn in requests, they are first spawned in a small space where these actions are the only options for the player. The "tutorial" has no explicit text telling the player what to do but simply leads the player through points of interest and text prompts. The request trees also help aid in tutorialization through narrative in the request texts.

Programming:
I implemented the UI system where the player turns in requests and bake desserts and the inventory system for the game. The inventory and requests are recorded through a scriptable object. I  was also responsible for implementing most of the events that exists in the game world and its triggers.

Narrative:
I implemented narrative in the form of request text and notes given to the player after a request was fulfilled.
